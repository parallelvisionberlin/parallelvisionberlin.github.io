Berlin 2063
Nebula does not govern through law.
It governs through probability.

Berlin is no longer one city, but a vertical archive of collapsed streets, night markets, data towers and forgotten metro stations.

Artificial intelligence predicts reality.
Synthetic guardians patrol the city.
Human memory is stored in the cloud.
Music remains the last unexplained anomaly.
Somewhere beneath Alexanderplatz, a child touches a field of sound and sees the end of Berlin before it happens.
The Anomaly
Camila was born in 1997, but Berlin 2063 remembers her as a statistical error. A woman moving across timelines. A presence Nebula cannot simulate. Every release in Parallel Vision opens another fragment of this future: the machines, the clubs beneath the city, the resonant frequencies, and the moments where reality begins to fracture.
