Parallel Vision / Living Archive

Berlin 2063

Berlin 2063 is an evolving audiovisual archive built from imagined documents, environments, characters and memories of a possible future.

The project uses fiction to examine the systems already shaping the present—from technology and architecture to nightlife, identity and collective memory.

Transmission 01

Infrastructure

The city continues to function long after its original systems have begun to fail. Transport, surveillance and public space remain active, carrying fragments of the society that built them.

Transmission 02

The City

Berlin has expanded vertically, but its historical structures remain embedded within it. Old monuments, transport lines and social rituals survive inside new technological systems.

Transmission 03

The Inhabitants

The archive follows individuals moving through this future—not as anonymous models, but as witnesses shaped by its architecture, materials and social conditions.

Berlin 2063 is not presented as a finished world. It is an archive that continues to remember itself.

Nina is one of its inhabitants—and the only one visitors can speak to directly.
