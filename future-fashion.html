Parallel Vision / Material Research

Material Research for Bodies in Transition

Fashion After Fabric investigates clothing beyond textiles through speculative materials, biological systems and responsive technologies.

Across its collections, garments become synthetic skin, protective architecture, memory systems and interfaces between the body and its environment.

The project exists across images, moving works, artist collaborations and future physical experiments.

Berlin 2063 / Central Thesis

From Garment to Organism

In the world of Berlin 2063, clothing is no longer an external layer.

Liquid-metal alloys respond to movement, temperature and bioelectric activity. Synthetic membranes merge with the body. Materials adapt, protect, record and transform.

The garment becomes a second dermal system: part clothing, part technology, part living structure.

Artist Collaboration / Moving Image

Worn by the Inhabitants

Fashion After Fabric expands through collaborations with musicians, performers and visual artists.

Artists are reimagined as inhabitants of Berlin 2063, wearing evolving material systems inside films, projected runways and live audiovisual environments.

The same garment may transform between bodies, performances and realities.

Future Development

Beyond the Image

Fashion After Fabric is an evolving archive of visual research, moving-image experiments and material propositions.

Its next stages move toward physical prototypes, installations, performance, projection and collaborations across fashion, music and technology.

The future of clothing may not be something we wear.
It may be something we enter.
